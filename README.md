# Good Neighbor Protocol (GNP)

> **Good neighbors bring casseroles, not chaos.**

Good Neighbor Protocol (GNP) is a handbook-backed framework for turning messy, vague, emotionally overloaded, or incomplete handoffs into clearer, more carryable requests.

It is a shared language. A collaboration protocol. A bridge between vision and execution.

---

## What GNP Is

- A **framework** for structuring handoffs and requests
- A **collaboration protocol** for teams, partners, and cross-functional work
- A **shared language** that reduces friction before a project even starts
- A **bridge** between the person who has the idea and the person who has to carry it

## What GNP Is Not

- Not a replacement for Jira, GitHub, Slack, Asana, Linear, ClickUp, Trello, or email
- Not a full software platform (yet)
- Not open source at this time

---

## The Core Idea

Most handoffs fail before they begin. The person making a request doesn't know what's missing. The person receiving it doesn't know how to ask. Everyone does their best, and it still ends up sideways.

GNP gives both sides a shared structure — so the request can actually travel.

**Clarity is kindness.**

---

## The CASSEROLE Framework

GNP organizes a good handoff around nine elements:

| Letter | Element | What it asks |
|--------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| **C** | Clarity | What exactly is being asked? |
| **A** | Alignment | Does everyone agree on the goal? |
| **S** | Scope | What's in, what's out? |
| **S** | Safety | What should not be touched or broken? |
| **E** | Expectations | What does "done" look like? |
| **R** | Reasonability | Is the ask actually doable? |
| **O** | Ownership | Who is responsible for what? |
| **L** | Limits | What are the constraints? |
| **E** | Explain Why | What's the purpose behind the ask? |

## Current State

GNP currently exists as a **handbook and framework**. This repository is its public home.

The framework is available for reading, referencing, and using in your own work. A future toolkit, GPT assistant, and app may follow — but the foundation is the handbook, and the handbook is here.
